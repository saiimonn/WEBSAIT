Computer Science as a Discipline

What is Computer Science?

The discipline known as Computer Science is generally well known throughout the masses as a study of computer hardware and software designs. Though it is true, there is more to it, however.

Computer Science is the discipline that plants the foundation for a variety of subjects, namely:

  • Computer design
  • Computer programming
  • Information processing
  • Algorithm solutions and problems

History of the Field

Computer Science has been around for quite some time, with the discipline itself being born in the early 1940's. But there have been notable works that have occurred way before the birth of this field, that may have laid the dirt on the field we are in. The history of this discipline dates back to the 17th century, but was taken more and more seriously during the early 20th century.

The earliest note of a computer (or a machine rather) was in 1673, when Gottfried Leibniz created the “Stepped Reckoner”, a digital mechanical calculator. Principally, he was considered to be the first computer scientist.

The central idea of a computer came from Alan Turing in 1936, when he created a computational machine that can compute anything, to which he called the “Turing Machine”.

Concepts in Computer Science

Computer Science, as a course, delves into the deep concepts needed to learn and master the skills needed for a job within the field. The earliest concepts learned in computer science include:

  • Algorithms
  • Computer Languages and its Concepts
  • Data Storage
  • Computing and Conversion
  • Human-Computer Interaction

What does a Computer Scientist do?

Computer scientists work primarily with software systems. The term “computer scientist” is not a job, but the discipline itself can lead you to numerous career choices, including:

  • Data Scientist
  • Web Developer
  • Data Architect

The 5 Computing Disciplines

What are they?

The field of Computer Science is not the only discipline in computing. T here are more computing disciplines and majors, which include:

  • Computer Science
  • Computer Engineering
  • Information Technology
  • Information Systems
  • Software Engineering

Career Paths

Computer Science: Explores theoretical and algorithmic foundations, and software development.

Computer Engineering: Focuses on designing hardware, software, and their integration.

Information Technology: Prepares students to address organizational tech needs (e.g., healthcare, education).

Information Systems: Integrates IT solutions with business processes to manage and distribute information.

Software Engineering: Combines math, computer science, and engineering to develop and maintain software systems.
